Nagz Parent and Guardian User Manual (V1.0)

Clickable web version for guardian onboarding and daily operations.

1. Who This Is For

This manual is for guardians who create, monitor, and adjust Nagz reminders for family members.

Back to top

2. Before You Start

  • Launch region is United States.
  • SMS reminders are US-only in V1.0.
  • Child accounts must be linked to a guardian-managed family.
Back to top

3. Core Terms

NagStrategyDone DefinitionIncentiveAI Mediation

  • Nag: A reminder task sent from one member to another.
  • Done definition: ack_only, binary_check, or binary_with_note.
  • AI mediation: AI-assisted status/excuse handling and summaries.

4. First-Time Setup

  1. Create guardian account.
  2. Create or join family.
  3. Add second guardian (optional).
  4. Add child members.
  5. Configure push/SMS preferences.
  6. Set quiet hours and daily limits.
Back to top

5. Creating a Nag

  1. Open New Nag.
  2. Choose recipient.
  3. Enter task details and due date/time.
  4. Select friendly_reminder.
  5. Select done definition and category.
  6. Attach optional reward/consequence policy.
  7. Review and send.

6. Monitoring Progress

  • Dashboard tracks due soon, overdue, completed, and miss streaks.
  • Timeline view shows created, delivered, AI updates, and completion/miss outcomes.

7. AI Mediation for Guardians

  • Recipients can submit status/excuses through AI.
  • AI produces summaries for guardians.
  • Tone options: neutral, supportive, firm.
  • AI push-back is bounded and audit logged.

8. Incentives and Consequences

  • Configure reward/consequence catalogs in settings.
  • Map trigger rules (example: miss_streak_3).
  • High-impact consequences require guardian approval.

9. Safety Controls

  • Block member interactions.
  • Mute/snooze reminder streams.
  • Report abuse from any screen.
  • Use relationship suspension for repeated misuse.
Moderation SLA targets: critical 4h, standard 24h, low-severity 72h.

11. Notifications and SMS

  • Push is primary channel.
  • SMS requires explicit opt-in.
  • Users can stop and re-enable SMS.
  • Quiet hours and daily caps are enforced server-side.
Back to top

12. Privacy, Retention, and Deletion

  • Encrypted in transit and at rest.
  • Operational events: 24 months.
  • Notification metadata: 12 months.
  • User content: deletion request or 24 months inactivity.
  • Audit/moderation records: 36 months.
  • Deleted account data purged/anonymized within 30 days (except required audit/legal records).

13. Common Guardian Workflows

Co-own policy: open policy settings, add co-owner, confirm approvals.

Repeated misses: review trends, verify history, apply/approve consequence, adjust policy.

Disputed consequence: open audit timeline, review trigger/approvals, amend and note.

Back to top

14. Troubleshooting

  • No push: check permissions and quiet-hour window.
  • No SMS: verify opt-in and US phone format.
  • No escalation: check daily cap and throttles.
  • No reports: only guardians can view reports.
Back to top

15. Best Practices

  • Keep tasks specific and measurable.
  • Start supportive, escalate tone only when needed.
  • Keep consequence catalog limited and predictable.
  • Review weekly reports and tune gradually.
